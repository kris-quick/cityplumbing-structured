Local Knowledge

Plumbing in Chorlton — What to Know

The Victorian terraces around Beech Road and the streets running south off Wilbraham Road have a plumbing profile typical of properties of their age: gravity-fed cold water systems, original lead supply pipes in many cases, and central heating installed decades after the house was built. Working in these properties requires an understanding of how the different elements interact and where compromises have been made in the past.

The 1930s semis in Chorlton Park — particularly around Nicolas Road, Merseybank Avenue, and the streets off Barlow Moor Road — present a different set of considerations. These properties were generally built with gas infrastructure from the start, but the heating systems are now 40 or 50 years old. We do a significant amount of full system replacement work in this part of M21, and also carry out a high volume of combi boiler conversions where back boilers are finally being removed.
