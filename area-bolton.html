Bolton's Trusted Plumbing Specialists

Bolton is a mill town with a housing profile to match. The BL1, BL2, and BL3 postcodes contain large amounts of Victorian terrace housing — some of the densest in Greater Manchester — built to house workers during the town's industrial peak. These properties have characteristic plumbing profiles: back boilers still in service, gravity-fed systems, and pipework that's been partially updated over many decades but never comprehensively overhauled.

BL3, around Great Lever and Daubhill, has a particularly high density of rental properties and a correspondingly strong demand for landlord gas safety work. We issue CP12 certificates across all BL postcodes and work with landlords and managing agents throughout the borough. Further out in Horwich, Lostock, and Westhoughton, the housing shifts to post-war and later semis where system upgrades and bathroom renovations are the priority.

Six Gas Safe registered engineers, all directly employed, all trained to the same standard. We give fixed quotes, confirm appointment times, and work tidily.

Local Knowledge

Plumbing in Bolton — What to Know

The Victorian terrace streets of central Bolton — BL1 around the town centre and into Astley Bridge, BL2 through Breightmet and Darcy Lever — contain some of the oldest continuously occupied housing in Greater Manchester. Back boilers remain active in a significant proportion of these properties, often paired with gravity-fed water systems and original cast iron pipework that has outlasted every owner who's occupied the house. These systems require genuine expertise rather than standard procedures.

Horwich, in the BL6 postcode, has a substantially different housing mix — semi-detached and detached properties, many owner-occupied, in good structural condition but with heating systems that are now approaching 30 to 40 years old. We carry out a lot of full system replacements and smart thermostat installations in this part of the borough, managing the project from survey to commissioning.
