Wigan's Trusted Plumbing Specialists

Wigan is a town where older housing and newer development sit side by side. Close to the town centre — Scholes, Newtown, and Poolstock in WN1 — the Victorian and Edwardian terrace streets contain housing that has often never had central heating installed as a designed system. Gas connections were added later, heating was installed in stages, and the result is often a patchwork that needs proper assessment before any new work begins.

Further out in Pemberton (WN5) and Standish (WN6), the housing shifts to predominantly post-war and late-twentieth-century semis. Pemberton in particular has a high proportion of former council housing where original heating systems — often from the 1970s and early 1980s — are now at or past the end of their design life. Full system replacement is among the most common jobs we carry out in this part of the borough. Standish tends toward higher-specification work: quality bathroom renovations and smart heating controls in well-maintained owner-occupied properties.

Six Gas Safe registered engineers, all directly employed. We give fixed quotes, confirm appointment times, and work tidily in occupied homes.

Local Knowledge

Plumbing in Wigan — What to Know

Pemberton, in the WN5 postcode, transitions from older terraced housing near the Pemberton boundary with Wigan town to newer family estates towards Orrell and Up Holland. Many older Pemberton properties ran electric storage heaters for decades and are now converting to gas central heating for the first time. We design and install complete systems — boiler selection, radiator sizing, pipework design — and make sure the system is properly balanced from day one rather than leaving it to the homeowner to work out.

Standish, further north in the WN6 postcode, is a more affluent part of the borough with larger family semis and detached homes. The demand here is for higher-specification work: quality bathroom renovations, smart heating controls, and attention to detail in the finish. We work in Standish regularly and understand what the standard of work needs to be in this part of the borough.
