Legal Requirement

Your Annual Gas Safety Check — Sorted

If you rent out a property with gas appliances, you're legally required to have a gas safety inspection every 12 months by a Gas Safe registered engineer and to provide your tenants with a copy of the certificate. This applies whether you have one property or a portfolio of twenty — long-term lets, short-term lets, HMOs and serviced accommodation.

City Plumbing carries out gas safety inspections across Greater Manchester and issues the CP12 the same day. We work with individual landlords, letting agents managing multiple properties, and HMO operators who need reliable, scheduled inspections they can depend on.

  • CP12 gas safety certificate issued same day
  • All gas appliances inspected — boiler, hob, fire
  • Single lets, HMOs and multi-property portfolios

How It Works

How a Gas Safety Inspection Works

Straightforward, thorough and completed in a single visit — including the certificate.

01

Book the Inspection

Provide the address and access details. We can work with tenants directly or via a key. Recurring annual schedules available.

02

Engineer Attends

Our Gas Safe engineer arrives at the agreed time. They'll need access to all gas appliances — boiler, hob, any gas fires. Typically 45–60 minutes.

03

Full Check

Each appliance is checked for safe operation, ventilation, flue operation and gas tightness. Supply pipework is also checked for leaks. Any issues are clearly documented.

04

Certificate Issued

The CP12 is issued digitally the same day. You'll receive a copy to pass to your tenants within 28 days (or before they move in for new tenancies).
