Local Knowledge

Plumbing in Oldham — What to Know

Werneth and Glodwick, in the central OL1 postcode, contain some of Oldham's most characteristic Victorian housing — dense terrace streets built during the town's cotton-spinning boom that have never been comprehensively updated. Back boilers are common here, as are gravity-fed cold water systems where the tank sits in a loft that may never have been properly insulated. In cold winters, these are the properties most likely to generate emergency calls and we respond to them quickly and competently.

Failsworth, at the southern edge of the Oldham borough where it meets the Manchester boundary, is largely 1960s and 1970s semi-detached housing. This is a different set of plumbing and heating characteristics — systems from that era that are now 40 to 50 years old, increasingly unreliable, and increasingly inefficient. Full system replacement is a common job in Failsworth and we carry out the survey, supply, installation, and commissioning as a single managed project.
